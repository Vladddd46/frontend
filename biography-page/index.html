Alan Turing

The man who hacked Nazi

Biography:

  • 23 June 1912 - Born in Maida Vale, London
  • Very early in life, Turing showed signs of the genius that he was later to display prominently.
  • Between January 1922 and 1926, Turing was educated at Hazelhurst Preparatory School, an independent school in the village of Frant
  • In 1926, at the age of 13, he went on to Sherborne School, a boarding independent school in the market town of Sherborne in Dorset
  • In 1928, aged 16, Turing encountered Albert Einstein's work, which influenced him a lot.
  • 1931 - 1934 studied as an undergraduate at King's College, Cambridge.
  • 1936 Turing published his paper "On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem".
  • September 1936 - July 1938 Turing spent most of his time studying under Church at Princeton University
  • June 1938 he obtained his PhD from the Department of Mathematics at Princeton; his dissertation, Systems of Logic Based on Ordinals, introduced the concept of ordinal logic and the notion of relative computing
  • During the Second World War, Turing was a leading participant in the breaking of German ciphers at Bletchley Park.
  • From September 1938, Turing worked part-time with the Government Code and Cypher School (GC&CS), the British codebreaking organisation. He concentrated on cryptanalysis of the Enigma cipher machine used by Nazi Germany, together with Dilly Knox, a senior GC&CS codebreaker.
  • 8 June 1954, Turing's housekeeper found him dead at the age of 41; he had died the previous day. Cyanide poisoning was established as the cause of death.
